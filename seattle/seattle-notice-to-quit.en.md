A notice to quit is a formal warning that starts an eviction path, but Seattle evictions still require a court process and are not immediate lockouts. [seattle-unlawful-detainer-guide]

Washington law defines legal grounds for unlawful detainer, which is the court process landlords use to seek eviction. [wa-rcw-5912030:RCW 59.12.030]

Read the notice carefully and record all deadlines, stated reasons, and required actions. Missing a response window can reduce your options later. [editorial]

Respond in writing, keep copies of all communications, and organize your lease, payment records, and any notices you receive. [editorial]

If you receive court papers, do not ignore them. Seek legal aid promptly to protect your rights and respond on time. [seattle-unlawful-detainer-guide]

Contact your landlord in writing as soon as you know you will miss a payment. Explain the situation and propose a payment plan or timeline. Early, documented communication demonstrates good faith and may prevent a notice to quit from being issued. [editorial]

Massachusetts offers state and local rental assistance programs for tenants facing housing instability. Check current program availability and eligibility requirements through the state's official rental assistance portal. [ma-rental-assistance]

Even if you receive a notice to quit for nonpayment, an eviction cannot proceed without a court judgment in a Summary Process case. You have the right to appear in court, present defenses, and request a payment agreement with the judge. [ma-mgl-c239]

If you receive court papers (a Summons and Complaint), respond by the deadline stated on the papers. Failing to appear in court typically results in a default judgment against you. [ma-mgl-c239]

Contact a legal aid organization before any court date. Legal aid attorneys can review your case, identify defenses (such as housing code violations that offset rent owed), and represent you at no cost if you qualify. [ma-tenant-rights]

Keep every receipt, bank statement, and communication related to rent payments. A clear paper trail is essential if you need to demonstrate what you paid or when a payment was received. [editorial]
